Unconventional Translation of C9orf72 GGGGCC expansion generates insoluble polypeptides

by Maria Chavez

Posted on August 26, 2014 at 6:00 PM

In a study published in early 2013, researchers from the Mayo Clinic found that C9RANT aggregates resulting from non-ATG (RAN) translation form in neurons containing the GGGGCC repeat expansion in C9orf72, which is the most common genetic cause of ALS and c9FTD.
