C9orf72 repeat expansions cause neurodegeneration in Drosophila

by Maria Chavez

Posted on September 5, 2014 at 3:20 PM

In a study published on September 5, 2014 London researchers found that pure C9orf72 GGGGCC repeats, but not stop codon interrupted RNA only repeats in Drosophila cause neurodegeneration

GGGCC repeats are the most common cause of ALS and FTD. Healthy individuals carry less than 33 repeats (usually 2), whule c9FTD/ALS patients carry between 400 and 4400 repeats.

The mechanism through which this repeat causes neurodegeneration is still unknown, but there are 3 main theories:

  • Toxic repeat proteins generated by non ATG (RAN) translation
  • Downregulated expression of C9orf72
  • The lack of C9orf72 coding mutations

The team of scientist generated a sequence containing the repeat expansion and a 6 base pair interruption containing one stop codon in the sense and the antisense direction every 12 GGGGCC repeats (a stop codon for all 6 frames) They also generated a stop codon RNA only repeat equivalent in length to the pure repeat and longer RNA only repeats up to ~288 GGGGCC repeats. They expressed the constructs in human neuroblastoma cell line and found that the formation of RNA aggregates was dependent on length for both pure and RNA only repeats.

Next, they generated lines of Drosophila carrying pure and RNA only repeats. Both pure and RNA only repeats generated RNA aggregates in Drosophila. They found that pure repeats generated dipeptide repeat proteins (DPR) [a dipeptide is a molecule that contains either to amino acids joined by a single peptide bond or one amino acid with two peptide bonds] and the RNA only repeats did not. Expression of pure repeats in the eye led to eye neurodegeneration, while RNA only repeats had no effect. DPR proteins were blamed for the toxicity.

Expression of pure repeats killed all flies by 30 days, while there was no effect seen with the RNA only repeats. To confirm that DPRs were to blame, they used cycloheximide (an inhibitor of protein biosynthesis), which decreased the lifespean reduction caused by the pure repeats.

To see if DPR protein expression was sufficient for toxicity, they generated protein only constructs. When constructs containing 36 DPRs were expressed in the fly eye arginine containing DPRs caused eye degeneration, while the others had no effect.

Conclusion: they found that arginine containing DPRs produced by the GGGGCC repeat expansion drive toxicity.

Mizielinska, S., Grönke, S., Niccoli, T., Ridler, C. E., Clayton, E. L., Devoy, A., … Isaacs, A. M. (2014). C9orf72 repeat expansions cause neurodegeneration in Drosophila through arginine-rich proteins. Science (New York, N.Y.), 1192. doi:10.1126/science.1256800
