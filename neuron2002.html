Unconventional Translation of C9orf72 GGGGCC expansion generates insoluble polypeptides

by Maria Chavez

Posted on August 26, 2014 at 6:00 PM

In a study published in early 2013, researchers from the Mayo Clinic found that C9RANT aggregates resulting from non-ATG (RAN) translation form in neurons containing the GGGGCC repeat expansion in C9orf72, which is the most common genetic cause of ALS and c9FTD.

The mechanisms by which the GGGGCC repeat expansion causes ALS are still unknown, but since mRNA levels of C9orf72 are down regulated, a loss of function mechanism might be the explanation. Another possible explanation is non-ATG translation, which results from the lack of initiating ATG codon.

The detection of nuclear aggregates containing RNA transcripts containing the GGGGCC repeat expansion in cortical and spinal cord neurons of c9FTD/ALS cases suggests that these aggregates might be available for RAN translation. To see if RAN translation products are indeed expressed, the team of scientist generated 2 anti-C9RANT antibodies. To determine the selectivity of both antibodies, they performed immunoassays.

To evaluate if RAN translation products are formed in c9FTD/ALS they performed western blot analysis on cerebellar tissue from ALS and FTD both with and without the repeat expansion. Anti-C9RANT aggregates were abundant in the cerebellum of c9FTD/ALS cases but absent in cases without the repeat expansion. Next, pre-mRNA from 2 c9FTD and 2 control cases was sequenced, and they found stop codons between ATG and the repeat expansion.

Next the distribution of aggregates was assessed. Neuronal cytoplasmic and intranuclear inclusions were found. The inclusions were exclusively found in gray matter and within neurons, and weren't found in vascular endothelial or smooth muscle cells, nor in white matter or glia.

To determine the specificity of the aggregates, they performed immunohistochemistry in 44 FTD and 6 ALS cases lacking the repeat expansion, as well as 65 cases with other diseases. In none of those cases they found C9RANT immunoreactivity.

Summary: they found C9RANT aggregates resulting from RAN translation in c9FTD and ALS but not in cases lacking the repeat expansion. This is relevant, as it sheds light on a potential pathogenic mechanism for the repeat expansion, and C9RANT aggregates might be used as a biomarker for c9FTD/ALS

Ash, P. E. a, Bieniek, K. F., Gendron, T. F., Caulfield, T., Lin, W.-L., Dejesus-Hernandez, M., … Petrucelli, L. (2013). Unconventional translation of C9ORF72 GGGGCC expansion generates insoluble polypeptides specific to c9FTD/ALS. Neuron, 77(4), 639–46. doi:10.1016/j.neuron.2013.02.004
