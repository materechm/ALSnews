Profilin 1 is associated with stress granules

by Maria Chavez

Posted on September 17, 2014 at 3:10 PM

Mutations in the gene encoding profilin 1 are a cause of fALS, but the mechanisms through which these mutations cause ALS are unknonw. In a study published in June on The Journal of Neuroscience, a team of researchers from Stanford University used yeast to try to gain a better understanding of the pathogenic mechanism of the mutations.

Profilin 1 is an actin binding protein that regulates actin polymerization. In 2012 the gene was found to be a cause of fALS.

The team of scientist expressed ALS-linked profilin 1 mutants in yeast. They found that the mutations cause a loss of function. Two mutations affected expression levels and protein stability compared to wild type.

Next they tested if the ALS-linked profilin 1 proteins caused a toxic gain of function. No toxicity was observed with profilin 1 variant overexpression, suggesting there is not a toxic gain of function mechanism.

Afterwards they performed an unbiested GWAS screen to identify gene deletions that became lethal when combined with the profilin 1 deletion. They identified 127 gene deletions that were lethal/sick in combination with Pfy1, including 6 of the 7 genes that regulate protein urmylation (attachment of ubiquitin like protein URM1 to another protein. This process might be enhanced by oxidative stress), and several genes encoding members of dynein and dynactin complexes.

A lot of genes encoding stress granule associated proteins were also found to interact with Pfy1 in the lethal screen. Notably, Pbp1 was found to interact with Pfy1. Pbp1 is a modifier of yeast stress granule formation (the yeast ortholog of ataxin 2)

Since a role for profilin 1 in stress granules had not been reported previously, they checked whether profilin 1 could associate with stress granules in human cells. They found prominent colocalization to profilin 1 with ataxin 2 positive stress granules in two independent human cell lines with 3 different stressors and 3 different stress granule markers. They checked whether profilin 1 and 2 associate with stress granules in neurons, and they found that both profilin 1 and 2 colocalize with ataxin 2 positive neuronal stress granules.

Next they checked whether ALS linked mutations in profilin 1 alter its ability to be cleared from stress granules. They found that profilin 1 may promote stress granule assembly and that overexpression of profilin 1 may cause a gain of function and.or dominant negative toxicity. They also found that profilin 1 is not required for stress granule formation in response to heat shock stress.

Figley, M. D., Bieri, G., Kolaitis, R.-M., Taylor, J. P., & Gitler, A. D. (2014). Profilin 1 associates with stress granules and ALS-linked mutations alter stress granule dynamics.
