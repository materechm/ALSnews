Mutations in UBQLN2 are a cause of ALS

by Maria Chavez

Posted on October 29, 2014 at 1:54 PM

Mutations in FUS, TDP43 and SOD1 account for about 30% of fALS cases, but the cause for the rest is still unknown. A paper published in Nature in 2011 shows that UBQLN2 is a cause of dominantly inherited chromosome X linked ALS and ALS dementia. This mutation seems to lead to impaired protein degradation.

FUS regulates RNA metabolism and DNA damage repair

by Maria Chavez

Posted on September 29, 2014 at 10:47 AM

FUS cytoplasmic inclusions in neurons and glia are a characteristic of ALS and FTD. These inclusions cause a dysregulation in RNA metabolism. A study published on PLOS Genetics in June 2014 shows that FUS targets RNA for pre-mRNA alternative splicing and processing of long intron containing transcripts, which are enriched for genes in neurogenesis and gene expression regulation.

Profilin 1 is associated with stress granules

by Maria Chavez

Posted on September 17, 2014 at 3:10 PM

Mutations in the gene encoding profilin 1 are a cause of fALS, but the mechanisms through which these mutations cause ALS are unknonw. In a study published in June on The Journal of Neuroscience, a team of researchers from Stanford University used yeast to try to gain a better understanding of the pathogenic mechanism of the mutations.
