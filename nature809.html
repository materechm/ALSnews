Mutations in UBQLN2 are a cause of ALS

by Maria Chavez

Posted on October 29, 2014 at 1:54 PM

Mutations in FUS, TDP43 and SOD1 account for about 30% of fALS cases, but the cause for the rest is still unknown. A paper published in Nature in 2011 shows that UBQLN2 is a cause of dominantly inherited chromosome X linked ALS and ALS dementia. This mutation seems to lead to impaired protein degradation.

The reason why I am choosing this paper is because a lot of people have showed me a press release that makes it seem as if this paper discovered the sole cause of all cases of ALS and people get really excited about it. That is not what the paper actually says so I decided to write about it and try to get the facts straight.

They identified a family with X linked ALS, including 19 affected individuals. Genome mapping identified an area with 206 genes as the area where the disease causing gene was. 191 of those genes were protein coding. The genes were analyzed and a unique mutation in UBQLN2 was identified (a C to A substitution)

To test if the mutation was causative in other patients they analyzed 188 people from families with X linked ALS or ALS/dementia. They found four other mutations in four unrelated families.

Next, to test if UBQTLN forms inclusions they performed immunohistochemical analysis of post mortem spinal cord sections from 2 patients with mutations in UBQLN2. They did find inclusions in the X linked ALS patients and they found that the mutations were immunoreactive with antibodies to ubiquitin, p62, TDP43, FUS and optineurin but not SOD1.

To test if UBQLN2 is involved in inclusion fomration in other types of ALS they examined 47 post mortem spinal cord samples, including cases of sALS, fALS without mutations in SOD1, TDP43 and FUS, ALS with demential, fALS with SOD1 mutations, fALS with TDP43 mutations and controls withut ALS. They found UBQTLN mutations in all ALS cases indicating it is a common compontent in a wide variety of ALS cases.

Dementia was a prominent feature in UBQLN2 cases and they also found ubiquilin 2 pathology in cases without UBQLN2 mutations, suggesting that ubiquilin 2 is involved in ALS related dementia, even without UBQLN2 mutations. Additionally, they found TDP43 and dementia linked ubiquilin2 are prone to co-aggregation.

Finally they tested the functional consequences of mutant ubiquilin 2 in protein degradation through the ubiquitin proteasome system (UPS). They found that the rates of degradation were significantly slower inc ells containing ubuiquilin mutations.

Deng, H.-X., Chen, W., Hong, S.-T., Boycott, K. M., Gorrie, G. H., Siddique, N., … Siddique, T. (2011). Mutations in UBQLN2 cause dominant X-linked juvenile and adult-onset ALS and ALS/dementia. Nature, 477(7363), 211–5. doi:10.1038/nature10353
